COMMUNITY HEALTH CLINIC — ANNUAL PROGRAM EFFECTIVENESS REPORT
Fiscal Year 2024-2025 | Prepared by: Quality Improvement Department

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This report evaluates program performance across four core service areas. Overall clinic performance shows mixed results, with significant variation in cost-effectiveness and outcomes across programs. Budget constraints have impacted service delivery, particularly in mental health where demand continues to outpace capacity.

---

DIABETES MANAGEMENT PROGRAM

Outcomes:
- A1C reduction achieved in 68% of enrolled patients (target: 70%)
- 312 ER visits attributed to diabetes complications (14% reduction from prior year)
- Patient enrollment: 847 active patients (capacity: 800 — over capacity)
- Diabetes education class completion rate: 54% (target: 75%)

Cost-Effectiveness:
- Cost per patient per year: $496
- Estimated ER avoidance savings: $234,000
- ROI ratio: 1.8x (every $1 invested saves $1.80 in downstream costs)
- Program is BELOW target on completion rates due to language access gaps

Key Finding: Spanish-speaking patient outcomes are 23% worse than English-speaking patients due to single bilingual staff member. Critical staffing gap.

---

HYPERTENSION CONTROL PROGRAM

Outcomes:
- Blood pressure control achieved in 74% of enrolled patients (target: 72%) — EXCEEDS TARGET
- 198 ER visits attributed to hypertension (22% reduction from prior year)
- Patient enrollment: 612 active patients (capacity: 650 — under capacity)
- Medication adherence rate: 81% (target: 75%) — EXCEEDS TARGET

Cost-Effectiveness:
- Cost per patient per year: $506
- Estimated ER avoidance savings: $297,000
- ROI ratio: 2.4x — HIGHEST in clinic
- Program is performing well; has capacity for growth

Key Finding: Telehealth pilot (Q3-Q4) showed 31% improvement in appointment adherence for elderly patients. Expansion recommended.

---

MATERNAL HEALTH PROGRAM

Outcomes:
- Full prenatal care completion: 71% (target: 80%) — BELOW TARGET
- Low birth weight rate: 8.2% (county average: 9.1%) — performing above county average
- Postpartum depression screening completion: 63% (target: 90%) — SIGNIFICANTLY BELOW TARGET
- High-risk pregnancy complications: 18% lower than county average

Cost-Effectiveness:
- Cost per patient per year: $348
- Estimated downstream savings (NICU avoidance, complications): $412,000
- ROI ratio: 2.1x
- Postpartum services critically underfunded

Key Finding: Postpartum care gap is significant. Patients are falling off after delivery. Single midwife is a single point of failure for the entire program.

---

MENTAL HEALTH PROGRAM

Outcomes:
- Patients served: 389 (capacity: 250 — 56% OVER CAPACITY)
- Average wait time for first appointment: 6.2 weeks (target: 2 weeks)
- Treatment completion rate: 41% (target: 65%) — SIGNIFICANTLY BELOW TARGET
- Crisis intervention calls: 127 (38% increase from prior year)
- Patient satisfaction: 2.1/5 — LOWEST in clinic

Cost-Effectiveness:
- Cost per patient per year: $501
- Unmet demand estimate: 400+ patients on informal waiting list
- ROI ratio: 0.9x (below break-even due to capacity constraints and drop-offs)
- NOTE: ROI calculation does not capture long-term social cost avoidance

Key Finding: Mental health is the most underfunded program relative to demonstrated community need. Low ROI is a capacity problem, not a program quality problem. Staff burnout is at critical levels — risk of losing both counselors within 12 months without intervention.

---

CROSS-PROGRAM FINDINGS

1. Language access is a clinic-wide gap affecting outcomes in all programs.
2. Transportation is the #1 cited access barrier across all programs.
3. Mental health unmet need is growing at 15% annually.
4. Hypertension program has the strongest ROI and room to grow.
5. Single points of failure exist in Maternal Health (midwife) and Diabetes (bilingual CHW).

RECOMMENDED BUDGET PRIORITY ORDER (QI Department perspective):
1. Mental Health — urgent capacity expansion
2. Diabetes Management — bilingual staff addition
3. Maternal Health — postpartum program expansion
4. Hypertension — telehealth expansion (already performing well)
